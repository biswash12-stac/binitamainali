Why Creativity Matters

Creativity extends beyond painting or crafting with glue and scissors; it plays a role in children's learning processes by allowing them to express themselves freely and foster relationships positively. According to Howard and Mayesky (2022) when children have access to open-ended materials and the liberty to investigate independently they gain confidence, cultivate resilience and acquire problem solving skills in their own ways.

"Enhancing creativity in children nurtures them to evolve into self-assured learners with problem-solving skills and a flair for imaginative thinking"
- Isbell & Yoshizawa (2016)

In early childhood settings, fostering creativity supports:

  • Emotional wellbeing
  • Social connection and collaboration
  • Physical development (e.g., fine motor skills)
  • Language and communication
  • Critical and creative thinking across all learning areas
