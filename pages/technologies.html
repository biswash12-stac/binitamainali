Innovative Technology Strategies

Creative Coding Without Computers

Introduce foundational coding concepts through unplugged activities like coding with arrows, robot pretend play, and sequencing games.

Simple Machine Explorations

Provide loose parts and construction materials for children to discover levers, pulleys, wheels, and inclined planes through play.

Digital Storytelling

Use age-appropriate apps to combine children's drawings, photos, and voice recordings into multimedia stories.

Developmental Benefits of Creative Technology

Problem-Solving

Develops logical thinking and persistence through design challenges and troubleshooting.

Computational Thinking

Builds foundational skills in sequencing, patterns, and algorithmic thinking.

Innovation

Encourages creative approaches to using tools and solving problems.

Creative Technology Activities

Screen-Free Coding

Use programmable toys or coding cards to introduce sequencing and directional language.

Ramp Engineering

Provide varied materials for children to design and test ramps, exploring incline and motion.

Digital Art Creation

Use tablets with drawing apps for children to create and manipulate digital artwork.

Integrating Technology Across Curriculum

Technology connects to all learning areas:

  • Math: Using digital tools to create patterns and graphs
  • Science: Documenting experiments with digital cameras
  • Literacy: Creating digital books and stories
  • Art: Combining traditional and digital media

Assessing Technological Learning

Document technology learning through:

  • Photographs of children's constructions and designs
  • Children's verbal explanations of their problem-solving processes
  • Collections of digital creations with children's commentary
  • Observational notes on tool use and innovative thinking
